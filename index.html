Acrossthinlines - Deepak Pakhare's Design Portfolio

Deepak Pakhare is a User Experience Designer helping startups shape products. He researches and teaches service design. He has strong visual design, writing, story-telling and illustration skills. He admits his graphic novel creation is going at a snail's pace but insists he'll complete it someday!

Deepak has also worked with larger organizations in internet media, telecommunications and enterprise including in two design consultancies in Singapore where he led end-to-end UX projects as UX Consultant. He started out as a Visualizer in an ad agency in Bombay in the late 1990's but quickly moved to doing New Media and Web projects.

Deepak is passionate about social impact through design and guides graduate students to conceptualize services around social themes in the Service Design course he teaches at the National Institute of Design, Bangalore as visiting faculty.

Featured → Yahoo! Nunchuk | 2009 - 2011


I moved from Singapore to join Yahoo! Bangalore in early 2009 as Principal Visual Interaction Designer. This was a Lead Designer role in the Advertising Products Group (APG) at Yahoo! The APG was tasked with addressing Ad Quality Management issues across the Yahoo Ad Networks. The 200 strong internal team of quality reviewers handled Ad Quality and worked with several standalone legacy tools. The tools allowed them to wean out objectionable ad material like porn, gambling etc. from the networks. However the tools were inefficient and were a nightmare in terms of usability. I anchored user studies to identify issues with core workflows, led co-design sessions and designed a robust integrated digital workspace for Ad reviewers.

About


I grew up in Chembur, a middle-class industrial township in Bombay. I was active in sports, Cricket was a passion and led my school and college teams as captain in local tournaments. I was always a voracious reader and loved comics which inspired me to draw and sketch obsessively, paving the way for a BFA (Applied Art) at the Sir J. J. Institute of Applied Art, Mumbai. While working as a Web Designer at a tech startup in Bombay around the time of the dotcom bust in 2000, I got accepted as Masters student to study Service Design at the Interaction Design Institute Ivrea, Italy. The experience at Ivrea provided me with an opportunity to travel the world, make friends and work with some of the best designers in the industry. It left a lasting impression on me and helped shape my career.

The 3 year stint in Singapore after my Masters was equally rewarding. I picked up some core project management chops, learnt to eat with chopsticks and travelled extensively around South-East Asia. I discovered along the way that I can be a good guide and mentor to younger designers. I started out disastrously as a public speaker but now I have a better grasp on conducting design workshops, presenting at design conferences and teaching. On a personal note, I am socially outgoing, not very religious but spiritually inclined. Love the company of friends and family, dig the outdoors, motorcycle rides, meditation. Adore dogs. And always look forward to some weekend cooking. I currently live and work in landlocked Bangalore with my wife and miss the heady aroma of coastal mangroves of Bombay and Singapore.
